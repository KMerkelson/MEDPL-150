"Emotive Eye"

This eye is meant to express disappointment, like a teacher looking down at a student trying to claim their dog ate their homework.

"Illustrated Environment"

I chose a picture of a tropical island in the ocean for this assignment. The curvy island seemed like an interesting shape, and I was confident I could use three colors because the mountain was covered in green trees like the island and the white patch of sky could be made the same color as the sand. I divided the image into four layers: "Trees," "Sand," "Mountain," and "Ocean" (which included the water and the white patch of sky.) For the trees, sand, and mountain, I traced the shapes with the Curvature Pen tool because there were a lot of curves and I didn't like having to drag with the Pen tool. For the Ocean layer, I covered the entire Artboard with a blue rectangle. Then I copied the trees, sand, and mountain and dragged the duplicates into the Ocean layer. Using the Minus Front Pathfinder, I was able to cut out the shapes of the elements in the foreground. To make the white patch of sky, I Merged a rectangle and right triangle (created from a square) together, then duplicated it and repeated the same Minus Front trick. Finally, I went back and manually adjusted any overlap or uncovered spots with the Direct Selection tool. In the final image, I used three colors--green for the trees and the mountain, white for the sand and sky, and blue for the ocean water. I believe the beach ended up thicker than in the original photo because I had a hard time keeping it from covering some of the trees.
