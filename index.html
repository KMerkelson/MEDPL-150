This is my creative statement.

My most prominent memory of image-making is taking a photo of a bloody pigeon corpse on the street. I have a deep and abiding anxiety about death, to the point that I cannot think about it, yet something about capturing this perverse experience rendered it manageable and understandable. As a writer, I use my short stories to do the same thing to the horrors of daily life. I break down and communicate my fears and hopes as someone who is trapped in a white, patriarchal, heteronormative society and constantly thinking about the all-consuming passage of time. I hope to use media making to enhance my storytelling ability, combining pictures with text to create a new experience and finding new ways to communicate my complicated feelings. Some influences on my work are memes, popular culture, and the fascinating in the everyday.
