THE PROPERTIES

Well, the good news is that the potion has the right properties. It will indeed make the drinker’s speech much more convincing. The bad news is that it’s nowhere near strong enough. The way it is now, you’re pretty sure it will last a minute at most when it’s supposed to work for a whole night. At least you can have a chuckle imagining it wearing off at the worst possible time.

The worse news is that it took you so long to figure out how to get the properties correct that you probably don’t have time to fix the strength. And anybody who looks at this sad excuse for a Draught of Correspondence can tell it’s all wrong.

Hmmm. There’s a thought. Anyone who looks at this will know what’s up…but you could very easily fix that, couldn’t you?

You scan your contract again. The Count promised you the money and the endorsement upon delivery of the potion. There’s no provision that says your payment will be taken away if the results of the potion are unsatisfactory. There’s laws against that, put in place through the influence of your Guild because too many men had unrealistic expectations about a certain potion meant to help them…feel virile again.

What is the Count going to do, take you to court for fraud? He’d have to admit what he was ordering, and then he’d seem untrustworthy to everyone he was trying to magically persuade—not a good look when you’ve already made a fool of yourself by relying on charisma you don’t have to win people over. And that assumes he’ll even figure out that you lied to him. Lots of things can interfere with a potion’s effects, like drinking coffee when your alchemist told you not to, Bernardo.

So…you could just pour some dye in there and call it a day. You could betray the professional pride you clung to throughout your entire career, even as it’s sent your business into a tailspin. You could live with the knowledge that not only have you stooped to the level of con artistry, but you only had to do so because you weren’t skilled and smart enough to just do your job right.

But will you?

Actually fix the potion
