WHO NEEDS TO FOLLOW A RECIPE, ANYWAY?

In a very real sense, alchemy is like cooking. You should follow the recipe, but you could make substitutions if you don’t have the right ingredients on hand. All you have to do is balance everything out properly, and the resulting potion will be just as good.

Of course, if you fail, then you might kill your client. But you’ve heard that people in the East consume poisonous fish as a delicacy, so your simile still works!

There are two aspects of any potion ingredient that are critical to the brewing process. The first is its strength, and the second is its properties. The first one determines the duration of the effects. Too little and it might do nothing, or wear off at an inconvenient moment; too much, and if the system shock doesn’t do in the client, he’ll be stuck with the effects for weeks. As for the properties, an error could result in anything from uncontrollably speaking backwards to vomiting blood. You’ll need to use the correct mix of reagents to perfectly replicate both aspects of the siren scales, or this will be a disaster.
