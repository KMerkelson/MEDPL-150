THEFT! THEFT! THEFT!

If you’re stealing from rich people, is it really stealing?

Yes, actually. At least in the eyes of the law. Sure, the merchant who owns this storehouse is wealthy enough to inspect his fleet on a flying carpet. Also, he’s an asshole who drops his trash off that carpet right onto your—ahem, unfortunate passerby’s freshly washed hair. But you doubt that will hold up as a defense in court, so it’s imperative that you don’t get caught.

Under the light of the moon, the guards’ armor glints brightly. There’s no way you’ll get through the front door without being beaten by their clubs. Even if you did, the frame is covered in protective glyphs that forbid entry by anyone without the corresponding keystone. More generalized wards adorn the windowsills, keeping away robbers and birds alike with a nasty shock.

Still, two alternative entrances present themselves to you. On one hand, there’s a small window on the top floor where the glyphs have faded away from the wear and tear of weather, and nobody has been attentive enough to fix them yet. On the other hand, not only is nobody bothering to guard the back door, there are no glyphs on it—or rather, there aren’t anymore, because somebody scratched them out. You suspect the security may be running a side business with their boss’ merchandise (perhaps they took too many falling bread crusts to the head). So what are the odds that anyone will notice some more missing items?

How will you break in?
